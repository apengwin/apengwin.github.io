Photos from the Provoke Collective


I discovered the work of Yutaka Takanashi a couple summers ago, and I was struck by the emotion and movement he captures in his photos. He and his cohort embraced roughness and imperfection in their angst towards US Occupation of postwar Japan, with their focus on are bure boke - blurry, grainy, out of focus. Just look at the first photo here - a photo of a crowded subway in Shinjuku Station. It breaks so many rules of conventional photography – the cutting off the face of the man at the top, leading lines that point directly out of frame, a general lack of a central subject – but it captures a claustrophobia and sensuality of the black coats.
